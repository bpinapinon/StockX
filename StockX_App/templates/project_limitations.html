Recognizing the Limitations of Our Analysis

Data itself is a human creation, so it's no surprise that there are limits to its usability. Extraction and manipulation to extract meaningful results is only the first step. One must consider not just the "how" but also "what" you use the data for. The limitations that come along with those decisions must not be overlooked once results are generated.

Limitations

Data Size

We only scrubbed for the 50 "Top Seller" and 50 "Most Popular" Nike & Adidas shoes. Things to consider with small datasets include:

  • Outliers in small samples can significantly alter the analysis.
  • Not enough samples to generate "train" and "sample" datasets.
  • Missing values have considerable impact on results.



Static Data

Data was scrubbed on a specific date. For our analysis to be useful by end users long term would require a time-series model.



Macroeconomic Variables

The state of the economy on the macro level will have an impact on the demand of luxury items. We've been in a steady bull market, consumers have more disposable income to make these luxury purchases.

Data Variable Inconsistency

One of the critical variables in our analysis was shoe color. Color schemes included "eclectic" custom-named colors. Normalizing this variable to a known color schemes was/is a very manual process that requires human analysis.
